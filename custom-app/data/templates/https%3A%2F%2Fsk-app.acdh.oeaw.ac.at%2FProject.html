The SemanticKraus Project

Connecting Kraus-Scholarship to the Semantic Web

The SemanticKraus project provides a reference resource containing research data from several projects on the writer, satirist, and cultural critic Karl Kraus (1874–1936). The project compiles and transforms biographical and bibliographical index data to create a comprehensive network of people, texts, and their interrelations. In detail, the data contains

  • biographical data, including birth, death, occupation, party affiliation
  • bibliographical data: published texts (literary, journalistic or other)
  • legal files (i. e. bibliographical data) and legal cases (i.e. event data)
  • text passages and mentions resp. intertextual references on texts

The source projects the data is derived from are:

  • Die Fackel online, a complete digital edition of the influential periodical Kraus edited from 1899 to 1936.
  • Karl Kraus Rechtsakten, a digital edition of Kraus’s legal files from the estate of his long-time lawyer Oskar Samek.
  • A digital edition of Dritte Walpurgisnacht, Kraus’s stance in the face of the National Socialist takeover in 1933, unpublished during Kraus’s lifetime.

All of these projects – which are in one way or another linked to the ACDH-CH – have amassed substantial amounts of index data. Die Fackel online, for example, includes an index of persons mentioned within the more than 22,000 pages of Die Fackel, amounting to a total of 16,900 names and 129,000 mentions. Kraus’s legal files are accompanied by indices containing the data of 1,700 persons and 2,100 literary and non-literary works mentioned or cited in the legal files (which by themselves amount to about 4,000 documents). The index of Dritte Walpurgisnacht – notably a single, voluminous work – lists 400 people and 1,100 texts (the majority of which are newspaper articles) that are mentioned or quoted in 3,000 text passages.

All of this data, although overlapping to a high degree, had been mostly uncorrelated so far. It was transformed to RDF, ingested into a triple store and provided with a user interface based on ResearchSpace as well as a SPARQL endpoint. The result is both a research platform and a reference resource offering users the opportunity for manual exploration as well as automated data retrieval – thus ensuring the integration of Kraus research into the LOD Cloud and serving the frequently expressed demand for open, reusable, and interlinked edition data.
